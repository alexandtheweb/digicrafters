Conductive Catch'em All

Next project

What works - and what doesn't? Try different conductive materials from around the house!

How does it work?

If what you're testing has just a little conductivity, you can turn it into a key with the Makey Makey. Imagine a circuit (circle) going from the Makey Makey, up the earth cable (in your hand) and through you, and then through the item you're touching, back to the Makey Makey board. That circuit sends a signal - and your computer can't tell the difference between a signal coming from the MaKey MaKey and a signal coming from an actual keyboard. Why not try ketchup, cheese, paint, or play-doh? Even people are conductive! Connect one person to ground, and another to an input, and you can trigger sounds when they high-five.

About us

DigiCrafters is a series of learning, discovery and making projects intended to introduce secondary school students to physical computing. The projects were designed to give anyone a fun, easy and creative way to discover computer sensing and I/O.

Who are we?

We are three banana-wielding MSc students, learning about human-computer interaction at the University of Sussex. In the Spring of 2013, as part of a module called technology-enhanced learning environments, we worked with the students and staff at the Self-Managed Learning College to discover the best way to introduce kids to sensors, microprocessors and circuits.
